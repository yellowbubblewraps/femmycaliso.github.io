Customer Support

Building a Support System from Zero

Aspect Health · 2024

The Problem

Customer support was entirely fragmented — emails landed in inboxes, replies were manual, and there was no shared visibility into open issues, response times, or recurring patterns.

What I Did

  • Implemented Zendesk as the company's first centralized ticketing system
  • Designed ticket routing rules, macros, and automated workflows to triage and respond faster
  • Consolidated all support channels (email, ad hoc replies) into one unified queue
  • Built internal knowledge base documentation so every agent could respond consistently

The Impact

Transformed a chaotic inbox into a structured support operation — improving context tracking, response efficiency, and team onboarding speed.

Automation

Automating Payment Recovery Workflows

Aspect Health · 2025

The Problem

Outstanding payments were being chased manually — support agents were spending time on follow-ups that had nothing to do with support, and customers had no self-serve path to resolve them.

What I Did

  • Designed an automated reminder flow triggered on outstanding payment events
  • Built customer-facing payment links embedded directly in reminder messages
  • Removed manual follow-up steps from the support team's queue entirely
  • Documented the process so the team could maintain and iterate on it

The Impact

Customers could resolve payments on their own. Manual follow-ups dropped significantly, freeing up support capacity for actual customer issues.

Automation

CSAT Visibility Bot: Intercom → Slack via n8n

Aspect Health · 2025

The Problem

Customer satisfaction ratings were buried inside Intercom. The team had no real-time visibility into how support was performing, and there was no shared space for the team to react and improve.

What I Did

  • Built an n8n workflow that listens for CSAT submissions in Intercom
  • Routed each rating (score + comment + customer ID + timestamp) to a dedicated Slack channel in real time
  • Structured the notification format for quick scanning — star rating, comment, and context in one message
  • Created a shared feedback loop so the whole team could see and act on satisfaction trends

The Impact

The team went from zero visibility to a live CSAT feed in Slack — making support quality a shared, visible metric rather than a number buried in a dashboard nobody checked.
